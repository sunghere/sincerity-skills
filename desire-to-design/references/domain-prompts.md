# Domain-specific Question Banks

Question banks for the taste analysis (Stage 3) and beyond, tailored per domain.

## Vocabulary adaptation

The 7-stage flow uses generic terms ("taste DNA", "core mechanic", "scope"). Translate them to the user's domain so the conversation feels native, not theoretical:

| Domain | "Taste DNA" becomes | Core unit | Scope axis |
|---|---|---|---|
| Games | fun DNA | mechanic | playtime + content |
| Apps / SaaS | user pain DNA | core workflow | features + integrations |
| Books / Content | reading DNA | narrative voice | length + research |
| Businesses | problem DNA | customer profile | market + ops |
| Music / Art | aesthetic DNA | voice / style | output volume |

Use the section for the user's domain below for question wording, probing categories, and common scope traps.

## Games

### Taste analysis questions
- "Name 3-5 games you've put 50+ hours into."
- "For each one — what was the *specific moment* you felt was most satisfying?"
- "When you said 'just one more turn/run,' what was pulling you back?"
- "What's a game in the same genre that you bounced off — what was the one-bit difference?"

### Probing categories
- **Numbers vs unlocks vs systems vs collection**: What kind of progression hits you?
  - A. Numbers go up (level, gold, stats)
  - B. New things open (areas, characters, options)
  - C. Inefficient becomes efficient (automation, optimization)
  - D. Filling collections (pokedex, museum, completion)
- **Session length**: 5min bursts vs 30min sessions vs multi-hour saga
- **Difficulty**: Mastery through repetition vs strategic puzzle vs power fantasy
- **Solo vs social**: Lonely zen vs competitive thrill vs cooperative joy

### System decision questions
- Deck/inventory size — fixed or variable? (Trap: "unlimited freedom" usually collapses to one optimal)
- Resource system — per-turn reset vs persistent vs depleting?
- Run length — short loops vs long campaigns vs hybrid?
- Death/failure — punishing vs progressing vs restarting?
- Character — one to deepen, or roster to vary?

### Common scope traps
- "Open world" with solo dev (don't)
- "Procedural everything" without designed seeds (feels random, not curated)
- "Multiplayer" as an afterthought (it isn't)
- "RPG" without narrative writing capacity
- 3D when 2D would work

## Apps / SaaS

### Taste analysis questions
- "What apps do you use daily that you genuinely enjoy?"
- "What's the moment you reach for them?"
- "What about them is *better* than alternatives you've tried?"
- "What apps do you keep meaning to use but don't — what's broken about them?"

### Probing categories
- **Frequency**: Multiple times per day vs daily vs weekly
- **Mode**: Active doing vs passive consumption vs reference
- **Setting**: Quick mobile moment vs deep desktop session
- **Social**: Solo tool vs collaborative vs community
- **Output**: Creates artifacts vs just experiences

### Customer pain analysis
- Whose problem is this? (Be specific: not "businesses" but "freelance designers in their second year")
- What do they currently do? (Spreadsheets are the default competitor for everything)
- How much does the problem cost them now? (Time × hourly rate, or revenue lost)
- Are they actively searching for solutions? (Google searches reveal demand)

### Common scope traps
- "Platform" instead of "tool" (platform = needs network effects, far harder)
- B2B without enterprise sales experience
- "AI-powered everything" without specific value
- Building before talking to 10+ potential users
- Free + ads model for niche audience

## Business / Product

### Taste analysis questions
- "What's a company you genuinely admire — not the famous ones, but one you respect?"
- "What businesses have you been a happy customer of recently?"
- "What's an industry you've worked in or know deeply?"
- "What inefficiency in your past work made you think 'someone could fix this'?"

### Probing categories
- **Scale**: Solopreneur lifestyle business vs scalable startup vs eventual large company
- **Customer**: B2B vs B2C vs B2B2C
- **Revenue model**: Subscription vs transaction vs services vs marketplace
- **Defensibility**: Network effects vs proprietary tech vs brand vs distribution

### Reality questions (be honest here)
- How many months of runway can you self-fund?
- Do you have a co-founder or are you solo? (Solo is harder but possible)
- Do you have any potential customers who already trust you?
- Is your motivation freedom, money, impact, or expression? (Each suggests different paths)

### Common scope traps
- "Disrupting [huge industry]" without insider knowledge
- Two-sided marketplace as first venture
- "Better Uber/Airbnb for X" without unique angle
- Building before any customer conversations
- Pursuing someone else's exciting idea

## Books / Content / Writing

### Taste analysis questions
- "What's a book/show/podcast you finished and immediately wanted more of?"
- "What was the *voice* or *feel* you couldn't get elsewhere?"
- "What did you read/watch/listen to obsessively as a teenager?"
- "What kind of conversation do you wish existed but doesn't?"

### Probing categories
- **Form**: Short vs long, serialized vs standalone
- **Voice**: Authoritative vs intimate vs experimental
- **Audience**: Niche depth vs broad accessibility
- **Purpose**: Entertain vs inform vs persuade vs comfort

### Common scope traps
- "I'll write the great novel of our generation" as first project
- Newsletter without consistent writing practice
- Podcast without distribution plan
- "I have so many ideas" — pick ONE
- Writing for general audience instead of specific reader

## Music / Art / Creative

### Taste analysis questions
- "Whose work makes you stop scrolling?"
- "What's the moment in their work that hits you?"
- "What's something they do that you can't articulate but recognize?"
- "What's *missing* from current work in this space?"

### Probing categories
- **Tradition vs innovation**: Mastery of form vs breaking form
- **Output volume**: Few masterpieces vs steady stream
- **Distribution**: Direct fans vs gatekeepers vs algorithms
- **Identity**: Anonymous craft vs personal brand

### Common scope traps
- Trying to develop signature style before doing the work
- Avoiding all influence (impossible and counterproductive)
- Producing for imagined critics instead of real audience
- Quitting before public reception data exists
- Building infrastructure (website, store) before having work

## Universal probes (work across domains)

These questions reveal something regardless of domain:

- **The one-bit question**: "What's the one-bit difference between X you loved and X you didn't?"
- **The hidden assets question**: "What have you spent significant time on that others haven't?"
- **The motivation probe**: "If you could only achieve one thing with this, what would it be?"
- **The frustration mine**: "What current solutions in this space *almost* work but fail at one thing?"
- **The taste vs market check**: "If this only ever reached 100 people but they LOVED it, would you still want to make it?" (Reveals whether they want expression or scale)

## Red flags during taste analysis

If a user can't answer taste questions specifically, they likely haven't actually engaged deeply with the domain. This is important information:

- **"I don't really play games but I want to make one"** → high failure risk
- **"I haven't read fiction much but want to write a novel"** → almost certain failure
- **"I've never built a SaaS but want to start one"** → less critical, but should validate

In these cases, suggest a "consumption sprint" first: deeply engage with 5-10 great works in the space before committing to creating.
